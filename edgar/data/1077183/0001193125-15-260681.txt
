<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenue for the second quarter was $24.3 million, an increase of 18% over last year. The PathLogic acquisition accounted for $1.9 million or
half of the revenue growth, and organic growth in the base<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">(1)</SUP> business accounted for the rest. Test volume in the base business grew by 21% and average revenue per test decreased by 11%, primarily
as a result of significant decreases in reimbursement for Fluorescence in-situ Hybridization (&#147;FISH&#148;) testing as a result of new FISH billing codes introduced in 2015. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Consolidated gross margin, including PathLogic, was 44.4% for the quarter, representing an increase of 300 basis points from levels reported
in Quarter 1 and a reduction of 510 basis points from last year&#146;s second quarter. The sequential increase was largely driven by a 9% reduction in average cost-of-goods-sold per test (&#147;Cost per Test&#148;) in the base business from Quarter
1. The year-over-year decrease was the result of the inclusion of Path Logic&#146;s results in the second quarter of this year and the FISH reimbursement declines. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Consolidated selling, general and administrative expenses (&#147;SG&amp;A&#148;) increased by 12%, or $1.1 million, from the second quarter of
last year. All of this increase was due to the inclusion of PathLogic&#146;s SG&amp;A expenses into consolidated results. </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Our laboratory teams are also performing exceptionally well. Service levels were extremely strong during the quarter, even with the

Productivity<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">(3)</SUP>&nbsp;and reducing supplies cost per test. We now believe that we can achieve a 6-8% reduction in Costs per Test in our base business for the full year 2015.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Mr.&nbsp;VanOort continued, &#147;We are also particularly pleased that there was no increase in SG&amp;A expenses in our base business,

million less FISH reimbursement, continued insourcing by one large client, and continued losses caused from the acquisition of PathLogic.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;While 2015 results continue to be significantly impacted by the draconian reductions to FISH reimbursement, it appears that CMS is
seeking to correct the 2015 reimbursement levels based on the recently released proposed 2016 rule for the Physician Fee Schedule. If the proposed reimbursement levels are maintained in the final 2016 rule, flow cytometry reimbursement rates will
see significant declines but they will be more than offset by increases for FISH and other test types. If the rule is implemented as drafted, we estimate that approximately $6 - 8&nbsp;million of the $12 million 2-year reduction in FISH
reimbursement would be reversed assuming most commercial insurances will follow Medicare&#146;s lead. After factoring in the reductions to flow cytometry and other proposed changes in reimbursement, we estimate that there would be an overall
positive impact to revenue of approximately $4-6&nbsp;million in 2016. After five years of annual price declines, this would be a welcome respite.&#148; </P>

America&#146;s premier cancer testing laboratory.&#148; </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Adjusted EBITDA&#148; is defined by NeoGenomics as net income from continuing operations before (i)&nbsp;interest expense, (ii)&nbsp;tax expense,
