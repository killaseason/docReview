 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Item&nbsp;5.02 Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain
Officers; Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers. </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On July&nbsp;23, 2015, Colfax Corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;) announced the appointment
by the Board of Directors (the &#147;Board&#148;) of the Company of Matthew L. Trerotola as the Company&#146;s President and Chief Executive Officer, effective July&nbsp;24, 2015. Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola was also appointed to the Board of Directors
effective July&nbsp;24, 2015, and to the Executive Committee of the Board. Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola succeeds Steven E. Simms, who resigned as President and Chief Executive Officer effective on that date. Mr.&nbsp;Simms remains a director of the Company
and will serve as a consultant to the Company until December&nbsp;31, 2015. Also on July&nbsp;23, 2015 Clay H. Kiefaber notified the Company of his resignation from the Board. Mr.&nbsp;Kiefaber will continue to serve as Executive Vice President,
Chief Executive Officer ESAB Global. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>Biographical Information Regarding Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola, age 48, most recently served as Executive Vice President of E.I. du Pont de Nemours and Company (&#147;DuPont&#148;) since November 2014,
with responsibility for that company&#146;s Electronics&nbsp;&amp; Communications (&#147;E&amp;C&#148;) and Safety&nbsp;&amp; Protection (&#147;S&amp;P&#148;) segments as well as Asia Pacific regional management. Previously, from February 2014 to
November 2014, he was Senior Vice President of DuPont with responsibility for S&amp;P and E&amp;C, and from September 2013 to February 2014 he was Senior Vice President of DuPont with responsibility for S&amp;P. Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola originally joined
DuPont in 1989, and has held roles in sales, marketing, strategy/M&amp;A and general management. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Prior to his most recent service with DuPont,
Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola held a variety of leadership positions at Danaher Corporation (&#147;Danaher&#148;). From 2012 to 2013, he was Group Executive&#151;Life Sciences and from 2009 to 2012 he served as Group Executive &#150; Product ID. From 2007 to
2009, Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola served as President of Videojet, a Danaher subsidiary. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>Employment Agreement with Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On July&nbsp;15, 2015, the Company and Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola entered into an employment agreement (the &#147;Employment Agreement&#148;), effective July&nbsp;24,
2015. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The term of the Employment Agreement is three years with automatic one-year renewals unless the Board or Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola elects not to extend
the term of the Employment Agreement by providing the other party with 90 days&#146; written notice.&nbsp;Mr. Trerotola&#146;s base salary is set at $1,000,000, which can be increased but not decreased by the Compensation Committee of the Board at
its discretion during the term of the Employment Agreement. In addition, Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola is entitled to participate in the Company&#146;s annual cash incentive program with a target annual incentive bonus of at least 120% of his annual base
salary then in effect. For 2015, the 120% target bonus is pro-rated. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Under the Employment Agreement, as a signing bonus in recognition of his forfeiture
of unvested equity compensation from his prior employer, Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola was granted a cash bonus of $3,000,000 and restricted stock units (&#147;RSUs&#148;) with a grant date fair value of $3,750,000. The cash bonus will be paid in three equal
installments, beginning with the first payroll following Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola&#146;s employment commencement, and then on each of the following two anniversaries of his employment commencement. The RSUs will vest in three equal annual installments
beginning on December&nbsp;31, 2015. In addition, the Company agreed to pay to Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola or to his former employer amounts that Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola is obligated to repay his former employer pursuant to a letter agreement between
Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola and his former employer, plus such amounts necessary to avoid additional income taxes to Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola. </P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Under the Employment Agreement, Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola was granted a long-term incentive award comprised of stock
options with a grant date fair value of $9,000,000, which options will vest in three equal annual installments beginning on July&nbsp;24, 2018 and performance-based restricted stock units with a grant date fair value of $4,500,000, which units will
vest based on Company performance objectives established by the Compensation Committee of the Board and will further vest, if earned based on the Company performance objectives, based on the passage of time in three equal annual installments
beginning on July&nbsp;24, 2018. The exercise price of the stock options is equal to the closing price of the Company&#146;s common stock on the New York Stock Exchange on July&nbsp;24, 2015. The performance-based restricted stock units are subject
to cancellation in the event the Company performance objectives are not achieved. The options, RSUs and PRSUs have a grant date on July&nbsp;24, 2015. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Employment Agreement provides that Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola is entitled to benefits available to senior executives of the company, as well as an automobile
allowance of $20,000 per year, and personal use of the Company&#146;s private aircraft or personal financial planning services in an amount not to exceed $100,000 in compensation income to Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola in any calendar year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">If Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola is terminated as result of Company&#146;s notice not to extend the term of the Employment Agreement or by the Company without
&#147;cause&#148; or he resigns for &#147;good reason&#148; (each as defined in the Employment Agreement), he will be entitled to (i)&nbsp;his base salary then in effect for 24 months following termination, (ii)&nbsp;an amount equal to 200% of his
target annual incentive bonus for the year of termination paid in equal installments over the 24 months following termination, (iii)&nbsp;a &#147;partial year bonus&#148; (as defined in the Employment Agreement), if any, and (iv)&nbsp;payment by the
Company of employer premiums for COBRA coverage elected under the Company&#146;s basic medical insurance programs for 24 months following termination or until Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola becomes eligible for coverage by another company or otherwise is no
longer eligible for COBRA coverage (the &#147;COBRA Premiums&#148;). In the event of a termination without cause or resignation for good reason, the terms of Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola&#146;s equity incentive awards are governed by the terms of the award
agreements and the Company&#146;s 2008 Omnibus Incentive Plan (the &#147;Plan&#148;). </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In addition, if Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola is terminated by the Company
without cause or he resigns for good reason within three months prior to, or within two years following, a &#147;change in control,&#148; (as defined in the Employment Agreement), he will be entitled to (i)&nbsp;a lump sum severance payment equal to
two times his base salary then in effect and his target annual incentive bonus in the year of termination, (ii)&nbsp;a &#147;partial year bonus&#148;, if any, and (iii)&nbsp;the COBRA Premiums. In the event of a termination without cause or
resignation for good reason in proximity to a change in control, the terms of Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola&#146;s equity incentive awards are governed by the terms of the award agreements and the Plan. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Employment Agreement contains confidentiality, non-competition, non-solicitation, and non-disparagement restrictions during the term of the Employment
Agreement and for certain specified periods thereafter. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Employment Agreement also provides for Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola to receive health insurance
commensurate with the benefits that the Company provides our senior executives. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Under the Employment Agreement, Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola will be nominated for
election to the Board as long as he remains CEO. Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola will receive no additional compensation for service on the Board. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The foregoing
summary of the terms and conditions of the Employment Agreement is qualified in its entirety by reference to the full text of the Employment Agreement, which is attached hereto as Exhibit 10.1. </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>Consulting Agreement with Mr.&nbsp;Simms </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In connection with the resignation of Mr.&nbsp;Simms as President and Chief Executive Officer of the Company, effective July&nbsp;23, 2015, the Company and
Mr.&nbsp;Simms entered into a consulting agreement (the &#147;Consulting Agreement&#148;). Under the Consulting Agreement, commencing July&nbsp;24, 2015, Mr.&nbsp;Simms has agreed to provide transition services and advice to the Board and to the
Company&#146;s management, in an amount not to exceed 50% of the average level of services previously provided by Mr.&nbsp;Simms as an employee of the Company. The Consulting Agreement has a term ending December&nbsp;31, 2015, and may be terminated
by Mr.&nbsp;Simms at any time. Under the Consulting Agreement, Mr.&nbsp;Simms will receive a monthly consulting fee of $25,000. The Consulting Agreement also provides that employer premiums will be paid by the Company for 18 months of elected COBRA
coverage from July&nbsp;24, 2015 under the Company&#146;s medical insurance programs if Mr.&nbsp;Simms elects to purchase COBRA coverage for himself and his dependents. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The foregoing summary of the terms and conditions of the Consulting Agreement is qualified in its entirety by reference to the full text of the Consulting
Agreement, which is attached hereto as Exhibit 10.2. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In addition, the Compensation Committee approved an amendment, effective July&nbsp;23, 2015, to the
option and performance stock unit (&#147;PSU&#148;) awards made to Mr.&nbsp;Simms on April&nbsp;28, 2014 to provide that (a)&nbsp;the options that would otherwise not vest until 2017 shall be vested as to one-third of the total number of shares at
July&nbsp;23, 2015, Mr.&nbsp;Simms&#146; final date of employment with the Company, and (b)&nbsp;Mr.&nbsp;Simms&#146; final date of service with the Company for purposes of the awards, notwithstanding his continued service as a director, shall be
July&nbsp;23, 2015. The foregoing summary of the amendment to the option and PSU awards is qualified in its entirety by reference to the full text of the Amendment No.&nbsp;1 to the CEO Non-Qualified Stock Option Agreement and CEO Performance Stock
Unit Agreement, which is attached hereto as Exhibit 10.3. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>Resignation of Mr.&nbsp;Kiefaber from the Board </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On July&nbsp;23, 2015, Clay H. Kiefaber notified the Company of his resignation from the Board, effective as of July&nbsp;24, 2015. Mr.&nbsp;Kiefaber&#146;s
resignation was not due to any disagreement with the Company and was done to permit the Board to maintain a majority of independent directors following the appointment of Mr.&nbsp;Trerotola to the Board of Directors. Mr.&nbsp;Kiefaber remains with
the Company as Executive Vice President, Chief Executive Officer of ESAB Global. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Item&nbsp;9.01 Financial Statements and Exhibits. </B></P>
