<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Other Events. </B></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Common Shares </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The following is a summary of the material terms of the Company&#146;s common shares. This summary is not a complete description of the terms
of the Company&#146;s common shares and is subject to and qualified in its entirety by reference to the Company&#146;s Articles of Incorporation, as amended from time to time, and Code of Regulations, as amended from time to time, and the Ohio
General Corporation Law. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Under the Company&#146;s Articles of Incorporation, we are authorized to issue up to 20,000,000 common shares.
As of June&nbsp;30, 2015, 7,843,578 common shares were outstanding and 747,964 common shares were held by the Company as treasury shares. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Each common share entitles the holder thereof to share ratably in the Company&#146;s net assets legally available for distribution to
shareholders in the event of the Company&#146;s liquidation, dissolution or winding up, after payment in full of all amounts required to be paid to creditors or provision for such payment, subject to the rights of the holders of the Company&#146;s
Series B Preferred Shares (the &#147;Series B Preferred Shares&#148;) and any other senior securities the Company may issue from time to time (together with the Series B Preferred Shares, the &#147;Senior Securities&#148;). With respect to the
amounts to be paid upon liquidation, the Company&#146;s Series B Preferred Shares rank senior to the common shares. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Prior to 2000, the
Ohio Revised Code provided that, unless the articles of an Ohio corporation state otherwise, the holders of shares of an Ohio corporation have the pre-emptive right, upon the offering or sale for cash of shares of the same class, during a reasonable
time and on reasonable terms fixed by the directors, to purchase the shares in proportion to their respective holdings of shares of such class. The law was amended in 2000 to provide that shareholders of an Ohio corporation do not have pre-emptive
rights to acquire a corporation&#146;s unissued shares except to the extent otherwise provided in the corporation&#146;s articles. However, as amended, the law provides that the provisions, as they existed prior to the effective date of the 2000
amendment, shall continue to apply to any corporation incorporated prior to the effective date of the amendment, until the shareholders of such corporation adopt an amendment to its articles expressly providing otherwise. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Because the Company was incorporated prior to 2000, and the shareholders of the Company have not amended the Company&#146;s Articles of
Incorporation to eliminate pre-emptive rights, the holders of the Company&#146;s common shares have the pre-emptive rights described in Section&nbsp;1701.15(A) of the Ohio Revised Code, as that section existed prior to amendment in 2000. These
pre-emptive rights do not apply, however, to any offering or sale of treasury shares or to any issuance of common shares to shareholders as a share dividend or distribution, upon conversion or exercise of share options or other conversion rights, or
under certain other circumstances specified in Section&nbsp;1701.15(A) of the Ohio Revised Code, as in effect prior to the 2000 amendment. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">As an Ohio corporation, the Company may, in the discretion of the Company&#146;s Board of Directors, generally pay dividends to the
Company&#146;s shareholders out of surplus, however created, but must notify the shareholders if a dividend is paid out of capital surplus. The Company&#146;s ability to obtain funds for the payment of dividends and for other cash requirements
largely depends on the amount of dividends that may be declared and paid by the Company&#146;s subsidiaries. Thus, as a practical matter, any restrictions on the ability of the Company&#146;s subsidiaries, including Civista Bank, to pay dividends
will act as restrictions on the amount of funds available for payment of dividends by the Company. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The ability of Civista Bank to pay
dividends is subject to limitations under various laws and regulations and to prudent and sound banking principles. Generally, subject to certain minimum capital requirements, Civista Bank may declare a dividend without the approval of the State of
Ohio Division of Financial Institutions so long as the total of the dividends in a calendar year does not exceed Civista Bank&#146;s total net income for that year combined with its retained net income for the two preceding years. The ability of the
Company&#146;s subsidiaries to pay dividends is also subject to their profitability, financial condition, capital expenditures and other cash flow requirements and </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Holders of Common Shares are entitled to one vote per share on all matters submitted to a vote of the shareholders. Under Ohio law,
shareholders have the right to make a request, in accordance with applicable procedures, to cumulate their votes in the election of directors unless a corporation&#146;s articles of incorporation are amended, in accordance with applicable
procedures, to eliminate that right. Cumulative voting allows each shareholder to multiply the number of shares that he or she may be entitled to vote by the total number of Directors to be elected and to cast the entire number of such votes for one
candidate or to distribute them among any two or more candidates. The Articles of Incorporation have not been amended to eliminate cumulative voting in the election of directors. </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">For purposes of Article SIXTH, &#147;related person&#148; generally means any person, entity or group, including any affiliate or associate
thereof (other than the Company, any wholly-owned subsidiary of the Company, or any trustee of, or fiduciary with respect to, any plan when acting in such capacity) that, at the time any business combination is agreed to, authorized or approved, is
the beneficial owner of not less than 10% of the Common Shares entitled to vote on such business combination. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Article SIXTH provides
that, when evaluating a business combination or any tender or exchange offer, the Board of Directors of the Company shall consider, without limitation: (i)&nbsp;the social and economic effects of the transaction on the Company and its subsidiaries,
employees, customers, creditors and community; (ii)&nbsp;the business and financial conditions and earning prospects of the acquiring person or persons; and (iii)&nbsp;the competence, experience and integrity of the acquiring person or persons and
its or their management. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Article SIXTH further provides that the affirmative vote of the holders of not less than 80% of each class of
the Company common shares entitled to vote on the transaction shall be required for the approval of any business combination in which a related person has an interest (except proportionately as a shareholder); provided, however, that the 80% voting
requirement shall not be applicable if (i)&nbsp;the continuing directors, who at the time constitute at least a majority of the Board of Directors of the Company, have approved the business combination by at least two-thirds vote or
(ii)&nbsp;certain conditions relating to the fairness of the transaction have been satisfied. If the 80% voting requirement is inapplicable, any business combination requiring shareholder approval may be authorized by the affirmative vote of the
holders of the Company common shares entitling them to exercise a majority of the voting power of the Company in accordance with Article FIFTH of the Articles of Incorporation. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Article SEVENTH of the Articles of Incorporation provides that no amendment of the Articles of Incorporation shall be effective to amend,
alter or repeal any of the provisions of Article SIXTH unless such amendment shall receive the affirmative vote of the holders of not less than 80% of the Company&#146;s common shares entitled to vote thereon; provided, however, that the 80% voting
requirement shall not be applicable if such amendment shall have been proposed and authorized by the Board of Directors of the Company by the affirmative vote of at least two-thirds of the continuing directors. </P>
